## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Emily presents with sudden visual distortions and blurring of vision. [4][5]  
She reports decreased vision when reading and that straight lines/door frames appear crooked. [10][11]  
She also reports difficulty adjusting to dim lights when out at a restaurant with her husband. [13][15]  
She currently smokes about one pack of cigarettes per day and has had difficulty fully quitting. [22][23]  
Regarding blood pressure, she checks it when she can remember, and the clinician emphasized regular checks and keeping a log. [24][25][26]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Anterior segment exam: posterior chamber intraocular lenses (PCIOL) in both eyes. [26]  
Right eye: cornea clear, no MVI, cup-to-disc 0.2; fundus with retina attached 360° without tears/holes; posterior segment with drusen in the macula, retinal thickening, subretinal fluid, exudates, and hemorrhage. [26]  
Left eye: cornea clear, no MVI, cup-to-disc 0.3; no hemorrhage or subretinal fluid; retina attached. [26]  

## RESULTS
OCT shows drusen deposits at the retinal pigment epithelium with subretinal fluid accumulation and a visible choroidal neovascular membrane. [26]  
Fluorescein angiography shows focal areas of leakage in the right eye consistent with neovascular age-related macular degeneration. [26]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Neovascular (wet) age-related macular degeneration, right eye, based on exam and diagnostic testing. [26]  
The clinician discussed that this is an advanced stage that can lead to severe vision loss due to abnormal blood vessels growing beneath the retina and leaking fluid/blood, potentially causing a central blind spot. [26]  
Plan: start intravitreal Lucentis injections once monthly (after numbing the eye), with ongoing regular injections over time to slow vision decline or prevent further loss, though progression may still occur despite treatment. [26]  
Patient had no questions and agreed with the plan. [27]
