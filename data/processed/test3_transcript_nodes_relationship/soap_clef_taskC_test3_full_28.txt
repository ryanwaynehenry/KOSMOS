## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Mr. Thompson is a 67-year-old man with a history of essential hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and osteoarthritis who presents for evaluation of elevated and fluctuating blood pressures. [0]  
Over the last 3–4 weeks, he has had intermittent lightheadedness and slight headache, and home blood pressure checks have occasionally shown systolic readings >180. [3]  
He reports that when he checks his blood pressure during lightheaded episodes, it has still been on the high side. [5]  
He denies recent syncope. [6][7]  
He reports occasional mild chest heaviness but no sustained chest pain and no shortness of breath. [9]  
He notes mild unsteadiness during dizzy episodes and describes the dizziness as lightheaded/fuzzy rather than vertigo. [11][13]  
He denies fever, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. [14][15]  
He denies palpitations/heart racing. [16][17]  
He is taking lisinopril 20 mg twice daily and reports missing doses very rarely, using a pillbox and (despite some confusion) an app to stay on schedule. [18][19][21][23]  
He is also taking atorvastatin for hyperlipidemia. [26][27]  
His osteoarthritis is generally controlled; he remains active with golf but sometimes plays less or skips a day when the weather changes, and he uses Tylenol and activity modification for mild pain. [29][31]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM

## RESULTS
No in-visit laboratory or imaging results were reviewed; clinician planned lab testing (lipid panel, CBC with differential, CMP, and urinalysis). [32]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
**1) Hypertension with elevated/fluctuating home readings and associated lightheadedness/headache:** BP control felt to be out of control. [32]  
- Increase lisinopril to 60 mg twice daily. [32]  
- Start hydrochlorothiazide 25 mg daily. [32]  
- Home BP monitoring twice daily for the next 2 weeks. [32]  
- Follow up in 2 weeks. [32][38]  

**2) Hyperlipidemia:** Continue atorvastatin. [32]  
- Order cholesterol panel, CBC with differential, CMP, and urinalysis (including to assess kidney status). [32]  

**3) Osteoarthritis:** Continue to stay active (including walking/golfing) without overdoing it. [32]  
- Continue Tylenol as needed for mild pain. [32]  

**Counseling:** Maintain hydration (about 6–8 glasses of water/day; increase with heat or higher activity if feeling dehydrated). [33][34]  
**Alcohol:** Advised to limit beer/alcohol to no more than a couple drinks per week given BP fluctuations. [35][36]
