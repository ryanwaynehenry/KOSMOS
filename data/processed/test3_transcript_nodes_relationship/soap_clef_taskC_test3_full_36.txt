## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Joe presents for evaluation of a right knee injury with swelling and pain. [0][1][11]  
He reports the injury occurred while skiing on Saturday when he lost control in poor visibility, did a partial cartwheel, felt a “pop” in the right knee, and then had immediate knee pain. [3][5][11]  
Since the injury, he has been able to walk a little but notes persistent swelling and pain. [6][7][1]  
He has been treating symptoms with Advil, icing on and off, and occasional heat. [8][9]  
He denies any prior knee injuries. [10][11]  
After the fall, he also noticed his shoulder felt bruised, as he landed on that shoulder, with tenderness when raising the arm. [24][25][27][29]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Vital signs were normal: T 97.9°F, HR 64, RR 16, BP 110/74, SpO₂ 98%. [12]  
Right knee exam showed edema and ecchymosis with pain to palpation. [12][14]  
He had pain with flexion and extension but reported essentially full range of motion, limited by tightness and mild pain. [14][15][16][17]  
Shoulder exam showed bruising, and the patient reported tenderness with raising the arm. [28][29]  

## RESULTS
Right knee x-ray showed no fracture and no bony abnormalities. [18]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
1) **Right knee contusion after skiing injury**: Clinician impression was knee contusion with negative x-ray for fracture/bony abnormality. [3][18]  
Plan: rest and apply ice 2–3 times per day for 20 minutes at a time, and use NSAIDs such as ibuprofen every 6–8 hours as needed. [18]  
If symptoms do not improve, patient to return for reassessment and possible physical therapy. [18]  

2) **Shoulder pain/bruising after fall (concern for injury)**: Patient reports bruising and tenderness with arm elevation, and bruising was seen on exam. [25][28][29]  
Plan: obtain an x-ray of the right shoulder, with nurse to escort patient for imaging, then follow up to review results. [30]  

Patient had no questions and agreed with the plan. [19][31]
