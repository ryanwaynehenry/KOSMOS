Jaqueline presents after injuring her right ankle during a mountain biking lesson when she fell off her bike and twisted the ankle outward. [0][5][6][7][9][11]  
She reports pain primarily over the outer (lateral) ankle. [12][13]  
She reports some numbness but no tingling in the toes/other areas, and also mentions “numbing” in her heart. [14][15][17]  
She can move the ankle only a little bit and is not really able to walk on it, with limping. [19][20][21][24][25]

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Vital signs in clinic: T 98.6°F, BP 118/77, HR 77 bpm. [52]  
Right ankle: edema and erythema over the lateral malleolus with associated ecchymosis and a few excoriations, with pain to palpation. [52][53][54]  
Right ankle range of motion is decreased in flexion/extension and internal/external rotation. [54]  
No apparent injury to the knee or foot on exam. [54]  

## RESULTS
Right ankle x-ray showed no acute fracture or bony abnormality, consistent with a soft tissue injury. [54]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Acute right ankle sprain (right), with negative x-ray for fracture. [54][56]  
Place in an air cast and provide crutches. [56]  
Start meloxicam 15 mg once daily. [56]  
Ice the ankle for ~20 minutes five times per day and weight-bear as tolerated. [56]  
Expected improvement over the next couple of weeks; if not improving, return for reassessment and consider physical therapy and/or further imaging. [56]
