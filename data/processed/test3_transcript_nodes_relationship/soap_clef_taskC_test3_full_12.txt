## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Shirley Taylor is a 60-year-old right-handed female new patient referred by Dr. Jacob Ward for evaluation of bilateral hand numbness and tingling. [0]  
She reports numbness and tingling in both hands for a few weeks, which she noticed more after a motor vehicle accident last December in which she “broke them” (wrists) and they healed. [2][4]  
She has been wearing wrist splints (right and sometimes left), with the right side worse, and reports the splints seem to help. [5][6]  
She states symptoms in both hands have worsened over the last month. [6]  
Symptom distribution is described as right hand tingling/numbness from the middle and ring fingers up to the forearm, and left hand more in the pinky and ring fingers (not the middle). [8]  
She describes the hands/wrists as mainly sore with occasional throbbing and weakness, especially with use, and notes symptoms are worse at night and come and go during the day. [10][12]  
She denies clear loss of sensation (can still feel), though she thinks her hands may be less sensitive than they should be. [14]  
She was given a nerve pain medication but has not been taking it as prescribed. [14]  
She also reports mild neck stiffness and states she hit her head during the accident and was told it was a contusion. [6]  
Past history noted includes chronic kidney disease, gout, hypertension, anxiety, GERD, and diabetes, though the patient reports she previously had pre-diabetes that she controlled with diet changes. [15][16]  
She reports taking carvedilol 25 mg twice daily for hypertension and Lexapro (escitalopram) 10 mg daily for anxiety, and she has a sulfa allergy that causes a rash. [17][18]  
She denies any history of thyroid issues. [19][20]  
Review of systems was updated to include fatigue and weight gain; excessive appetite/always hungry and bloating; eye floaters; shortness of breath and leg swelling; skin dryness/itching and nail bed “trenches”; muscle pain and gout; and paresthesias in both hands. [23]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Neurologic exam documented as otherwise normal except diminished pinprick sensation in the right 3rd and 4th fingers and the left 4th and 5th fingers. [24]  
Reflexes were 1+ and symmetric with 2+ at the knees, and plantar responses were downgoing bilaterally. [24]  
Tinel’s sign was positive at both wrists and both elbows. [24]  

## RESULTS
CT brain with and without contrast from 2/5/21 was personally reviewed and showed no metastatic disease and no acute intracranial abnormalities. [0]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome with question of bilateral cubital tunnel syndrome. [25]  
Plan to schedule EMG/NCV for further evaluation and have the patient return for these studies. [25]  
If symptoms do not resolve, consider referral to hand clinic for possible symptomatic injections or carpal tunnel release surgery, noting she may also pursue these options through her current orthopedist. [25]  
Encouraged continued use of wrist splints. [25]
