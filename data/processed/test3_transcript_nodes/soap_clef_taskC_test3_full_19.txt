## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Emily presents with sudden visual distortions and blurring of vision. [4][5]  
She reports decreased vision when reading and notes that straight lines and door frames appear crooked. [10][11][14][15]  
She also has trouble adjusting to dim lights when out at a restaurant with her husband. [15]  
She currently smokes about a pack of cigarettes per day and has found it difficult to quit completely despite trying at times. [22][23]  
Regarding blood pressure, she checks it when she can remember to do so, and the clinician emphasized the importance of regular checks and keeping a log. [24][25][26]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Anterior segment exam: posterior chamber intraocular lenses present in both eyes. [26]  
Right eye: cornea clear, no MVI, cup-to-disc 0.2; retina attached 360° without tears/holes; posterior segment notable for drusen in the macula with retinal thickening, subretinal fluid, exudates, and hemorrhage. [26]  
Left eye: cornea clear, no MVI, cup-to-disc 0.3; no hemorrhage or subretinal fluid; retina attached. [26]  

## RESULTS
OCT shows drusen deposits at the retinal pigment epithelium with subretinal fluid accumulation and a visible choroidal neovascular membrane. [26]  
Fluorescein angiography shows focal areas of leakage in the right eye consistent with neovascular age-related macular degeneration. [26]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Neovascular (wet) age-related macular degeneration of the right eye, supported by exam findings (drusen, subretinal fluid, exudates, hemorrhage) and OCT/fluorescein angiography results. [26]  
Discussed that this advanced condition can lead to severe vision loss due to abnormal blood vessels growing beneath the retina that leak fluid and blood, potentially causing a central blind spot. [26]  
Plan: start intravitreal Lucentis injections once monthly (after numbing the eye), with the goal to slow vision decline or prevent further vision loss, though progression may still occur despite treatment. [26]  
Patient had no questions and agreed with the plan. [27]
