## RESULTS
MRI reviewed shows disc desiccation and disc bulge with paracentral disc herniation resulting in moderate right neural foraminal stenosis. [80]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Chronic lumbar back pain with radiculopathy, consistent with L4–L5 herniated disc based on symptoms, exam, and MRI findings. [17][19][78][80][82]  
Discussed that the disc bulge is pinching nerves, explaining leg tingling/numbness and pain with palpation. [19][80][82]  
Recommend fluoroscopy-guided spinal corticosteroid injection to target inflammation and pain. [82][92]  
Because of needle anxiety, plan to prescribe a medication to take about 3 hours before the injection appointment to help him feel calm enough for the procedure. [83][84]  
Pain medications: advised he should not need additional pain pills after the injection, but if pain persists after about a day, they will discuss pain medication options; clinician noted pain meds are not a good long-term solution. [92][96]  
If symptoms persist despite injection and medication trial, surgery may be considered, with intent to try other options first. [96][98]  
Patient to schedule the injection at the front desk; procedure is expected to take about an hour and, if effective, would be limited to about four times per year. [103][104]  
Patient was reassured he is not dying. [79][80]
