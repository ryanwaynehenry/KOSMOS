Jaqueline presents with right ankle pain after falling off her bike during a mountain biking lesson, with the ankle twisting outward. [0][5][6][7][9][11]  
Pain is worst over the outer (lateral) ankle. [12][13]  
She reports limited ability to move the ankle, is not really able to walk on it, and is limping. [19][20][21][25]  
She took Tylenol without relief. [26][27][31]  
She heard a “crack/crackle” at the time of injury. [32][33][34][35]  
When asked about fever/chills, she stated she has had a “fever,” but described it as “like two hundred,” and she was afebrile in clinic. [48][49][51][52]  
She endorsed numbness (and initially gave an unclear response regarding tingling) and mentioned numbness “in other places,” including “my heart.” [14][15][16][17]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Vitals: T 98.6°F, BP 118/77, HR 77 bpm. [52]  
Right ankle: edema and erythema over the lateral malleolus with associated ecchymosis and a few excoriations, with pain to palpation. [52][53][54]  
Right ankle ROM: decreased flexion/extension and decreased internal/external rotation. [54]  
No apparent injury to the knee or foot on exam. [54]  

## RESULTS
Right ankle x-ray showed no acute fracture or bony abnormality, consistent with soft tissue injury. [54]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Acute right ankle sprain after inversion/eversion injury while mountain biking (twisted outward) with negative x-ray for fracture. [11][54][56]  
Place in an air cast and provide crutches. [56]  
Start meloxicam 15 mg once daily. [56]  
Ice the area for ~20 minutes five times per day and weight-bear as tolerated. [56]  
Expected improvement over the next couple of weeks; if not improving, return for reassessment and consider physical therapy and/or further imaging. [56]
