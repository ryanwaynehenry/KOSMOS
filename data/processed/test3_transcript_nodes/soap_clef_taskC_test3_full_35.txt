## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Matthew presents for evaluation of vision changes, with particular trouble in the right eye. [2]  
He reports difficulty adjusting to dark/dim environments, especially when eating out at night. [3][5]  
He also reports decreased vision when reading, especially in darker settings. [6]  
He notes that straight lines (e.g., door frames and the grooves in his office paneling) appear crooked/wavy, which has been worsening over the last 3–4 months. [6][7]  
He reports current cigarette smoking of about 1 pack per day and has had difficulty quitting despite trying hypnosis, lozenges, and chewing gum. [16][17]  
He has a history of high blood pressure and reports he has not taken his blood pressure medication for a while because he forgets (pill bottle kept in a cupboard/back corner). [18][19]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Eye exam (anterior segment) showed posterior chamber intraocular lenses in both eyes. [22]  
Right eye: cornea clear; no neovascularization of the iris noted; cup-to-disc ratio 0.2; retina attached 360° with no holes/tears; RPE changes in the fovea. [22]  
Right macula/posterior segment: drusen in the macula with retinal thickening, subretinal fluid, exudate accumulation, and hemorrhage. [22]  
Left eye: cornea clear; cup-to-disc ratio 0.3; RPE changes in the fovea; no hemorrhage or subretinal fluid noted; retina attached appropriately. [22]  

## RESULTS
OCT showed drusen deposits under the RPE and subretinal fluid, with SRF accumulation and a choroidal neovascular membrane visualized. [22]  
Fluorescein angiography showed a focal area of leakage in the right eye consistent with neovascular age-related macular degeneration. [22]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Neovascular (wet) age-related macular degeneration, right eye, with subretinal fluid and hemorrhage, consistent with the patient’s metamorphopsia and decreased vision. [6][7][22]  
The clinician discussed that this condition can progress to severe central vision loss due to abnormal blood vessels growing beneath the retina that leak fluid and blood, creating a central blind spot. [22]  
Plan: initiate intravitreal Lucentis injections once monthly, with the eye numbed beforehand to minimize pain, and proceed with the first injection today after nursing prep. [22][32]  
Tobacco use: advised to cut back or quit smoking altogether. [16][18]  
Hypertension with medication nonadherence: instructed to restart taking blood pressure medication as prescribed starting today and to place the pill bottle on the counter to help with remembering. [19][20]  
Patient’s questions/concerns were addressed regarding injection discomfort, and he agreed to proceed as long as it will not hurt a lot. [28][29]
