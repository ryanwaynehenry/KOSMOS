## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Joe presents with a right knee injury with swelling and pain. [0][1]  
He reports the injury occurred while skiing on Saturday when his skis caught in the snow, he partially “cartwheeled,” felt a pop in the right knee, and then had immediate pain. [3][5][11]  
Since the injury, he has been able to walk a little but notes persistent swelling and pain. [6][7]  
He has been treating symptoms with Advil, icing on and off, and occasional heat. [9]  
He denies any prior knee injuries and confirms this is the right knee. [10][11]  
He also reports mild shoulder bruising after landing on his lead shoulder during the fall, with tenderness when raising the arm. [24][25][27][29]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Vital signs were normal: T 97.9°F, HR 64, RR 16, BP 110/74, SpO2 98%. [12]  
Right knee exam showed edema and ecchymosis with tenderness to palpation. [12][14]  
He had some pain with knee flexion and extension but reported near-full range of motion with tightness. [14][15][16][17]  
Shoulder exam showed bruising, and the patient reported tenderness with raising the arm. [28][29]  

## RESULTS
Knee x-ray showed no fracture and no bony abnormalities. [18]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
1) **Right knee contusion after skiing injury** — diagnosed based on exam and negative x-ray. [3][12][18]  
Plan: rest and apply ice 2–3 times/day for 20 minutes, and use NSAIDs such as ibuprofen every 6–8 hours as needed. [18]  
If not improving, patient should return for reassessment and possible physical therapy. [18]  

2) **Shoulder bruising/tenderness after fall (concern for injury)**. [25][27][29]  
Plan: obtain an x-ray of the right shoulder, with nursing assistance, and then follow up to review results. [30]  
The patient agreed with the plan and had no questions. [19][31]
