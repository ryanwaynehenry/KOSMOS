## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Mr. Cook is a 60-year-old right-handed male referred for evaluation of numbness and tingling in both hands. [0][2][3]  
He reports numbness/tingling for a few weeks, which he noticed more after his wrists healed following a motor vehicle accident last December in which he broke both wrists (right was worse). [2][4][6]  
He wears a splint on the right wrist and sometimes on the left, and reports the splint helps, but symptoms in both hands have worsened over the last month. [5][6]  
Symptom distribution: on the right, tingling/numbness extends from the middle and ring fingers up the forearm; on the left, symptoms extend from the pinky and ring fingers. [8][9]  
He describes the hands/wrists as mainly sore with occasional throbbing and subjective weakness, especially with use, and tingling is worse when pain is worse and becomes dull when pain improves. [10]  
Symptoms are worse at night and come and go throughout the day. [12]  
He denies clear loss of sensation to touch but feels his hands may be less sensitive than they should be, and he has not been taking a prescribed nerve pain medication as directed. [14]  
He also reports mild neck stiffness and a prior head contusion from the accident. [6]  
Past history includes hypertension treated with Coreg and anxiety treated with Lexapro, and he reports prior prediabetes now controlled with diet; he denies thyroid disease. [15][16][17][18]  
Review of systems is notable for fatigue, weight gain, excessive appetite/bloating, eye floaters, shortness of breath, leg swelling, skin/nail changes (dryness/itching), gout, and muscle pain, with the remainder negative per patient. [20][21]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Neurologic sensory testing showed pinprick sensation diminished in the right 3rd and 4th fingers. [22][23][24]  
On the left, sensation was decreased in the 4th and 5th fingers. [25][26][27]  
Reflexes were 1+ and symmetric with 2+ knees, and toes were downgoing bilaterally. [28]  
Tinel sign was positive at both wrists and both elbows, and the remainder of the exam was normal. [29][30][31][32][33]  

## RESULTS
In-office neurologic exam findings included decreased pinprick sensation in the right 3rd/4th digits and decreased sensation in the left 4th/5th digits. [24][27]  
Tinel testing produced paresthesias and was positive at bilateral wrists and elbows. [29][30][31][32][33]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Bilateral hand numbness/tingling after prior wrist fractures from an MVA, with exam findings concerning for bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome versus bilateral cubital tunnel syndrome (presentation felt more consistent with carpal tunnel). [4][6][33][34]  
Plan to obtain EMG/nerve conduction testing to localize the site of nerve irritation. [36]  
Patient will return for follow-up after EMG results are available. [38]  
If symptoms persist, consider referral to hand clinic or orthopedics for possible injections or carpal tunnel release surgery. [38]  
Continue wearing wrist splints. [40]
