## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Shirley Taylor is a 60-year-old right-handed female, new patient referred by Dr. Jacob Ward for evaluation of bilateral hand numbness and tingling. [0]  
She reports numbness and tingling in both hands for a few weeks, with a rough weekend due to symptoms. [2][3][4]  
Symptoms began after a motor vehicle accident last December in which she broke both wrists, and she noticed the tingling/numbness more once the fractures healed. [4]  
She wears wrist splints (right worse than left) and sometimes wears the left splint as well; splinting seems to help, but symptoms in both hands have worsened over the last month. [5][6]  
On the right, she notices tingling/numbness from the middle and ring fingers up to the forearm, while on the left it is more in the pinky and ring fingers (not the middle). [8]  
She describes the hands as mainly sore with occasional throbbing and weakness, especially with use, and notes symptoms are worse at night and come and go during the day. [10][11][12]  
She denies clear loss of sensation (can still feel), though she thinks her hands may be less sensitive than they should be. [14]  
She was given a nerve pain medication but has not been taking it as prescribed. [14][21]  
She also reports mild neck stiffness and states she hit her head during the accident and was told it was a contusion. [6]  
Past history noted includes chronic kidney disease, gout, hypertension, anxiety, GERD, and diabetes; patient clarifies she had pre-diabetes that she got under control with diet changes. [15][16]  
She continues carvedilol 25 mg twice daily for hypertension and Lexapro 10 mg daily for anxiety, and reports a sulfa allergy causing rash. [17][18]  
She reports no history of thyroid issues. [19][20]  
Review of systems was updated to include fatigue, weight gain, excessive appetite/bloating, eye floaters, shortness of breath, leg swelling, skin/nail changes (nail bed trenches, dryness, itching), muscle pain, gout, and paresthesias in both hands. [23]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Neurologic exam: sensation normal except diminished pinprick in the 3rd and 4th fingers on the right and the 4th and 5th fingers on the left. [24]  
Reflexes were 1+ and symmetric with 2+ at the knees, and plantar responses were downgoing bilaterally. [24]  
Tinel’s sign was positive at both wrists and both elbows. [24]  

## RESULTS
CT brain with and without contrast dated 2/5/21 was personally reviewed and showed no metastatic disease and no acute intracranial abnormalities. [0]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome; question of bilateral cubital tunnel syndrome. [25]  
Plan to schedule EMG/NCV for further evaluation. [25]  
If symptoms do not resolve, consider hand clinic referral for possible symptomatic injections or carpal tunnel release surgery, which she may also pursue through her current orthopedist. [25]  
Encouraged continued splint use and to return for EMG/NCV. [25]
