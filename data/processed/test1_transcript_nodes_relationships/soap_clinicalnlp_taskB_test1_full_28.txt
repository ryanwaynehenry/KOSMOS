## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Bobby presents with right shoulder pain for the past few weeks after doing extensive backyard/yard work and projects. [2][3][21]  
He reports a prior right shoulder injury from playing baseball when he was younger and has had some baseline issues since then. [5]  
Current pain is described as more on the outside of the shoulder with tenderness, worse when raising/moving the arm, and minimal/no pain at rest. [7][11]  
He reports no restriction in arm movement but does have pain with movement, and it bothers him somewhat at night because he sleeps on that side. [9][13]  
He has been taking Advil and using ice, which helps a little but symptoms persist. [17][19]  
He denies numbness in the arm or hands. [24][25]  
He also reports bilateral foot soreness/stiffness (including ankle joints) after doing a lot of yard work, with some improvement using elevation and ice. [33]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Vitals reviewed and were normal: T 97.8, HR 62, RR 16, BP 116/84, SpO2 99%, and no fever. [26]  
Right shoulder exam showed limited active and passive range of motion with tenderness at the greater tuberosity of the humerus and no tenderness at the sternoclavicular or acromioclavicular joints. [26]  
Grip strength was good. [26]  
Right upper extremity neurovascular exam was intact with capillary refill <3 seconds and intact sensation to touch. [26]  

## RESULTS
Shoulder X-ray was reviewed and showed no fracture. [26]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Right shoulder pain: clinician assessed symptoms as most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy. [26]  
Plan includes ordering an MRI given prior shoulder problems to evaluate for other pathology. [26]  
Refer to physical therapy for approximately 6–8 weeks. [26][28]  
Continue Tylenol for symptom control during PT, and if not improving consider a steroid injection for pain relief. [26]  
Bilateral foot soreness/stiffness after yard work: if no specific injury is suspected, clinician recommended soaking feet in Epsom salts to help relax muscles and reduce soreness. [33][34]  
Patient agreed with the shoulder plan and had no questions, and also agreed to try the Epsom salt soaks. [27][35]
