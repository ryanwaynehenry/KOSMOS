## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Mrs. Martinez is a 43-year-old female presenting with pain in the distal radius/toward the wrist after a tennis injury. [2][3][4][7]  
She reports she missed a backhand and struck the net/pole, which jarred her arm. [7]  
She had a small amount of swelling. [8][9]  
She has pain with wrist movement and denies any numbness in the hand. [10][11]  
Past medical history is notable for allergies treated with Flonase. [12][13]  
She reports a prior injury when she fell during lawn work onto a rake. [14][15][17]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
There is tenderness to palpation over the distal radius. [24][25][36]  
Pain is elicited with bending the arm and with stressing/flexing the thumb, with less pain on extension/backward movement. [26][27][30][31][32][33][36]  

## RESULTS
Arm x-ray shows no fracture and no abnormalities (essentially normal). [22]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Distal radius/forearm pain after tennis injury, assessed as strain with possible contusion; fracture ruled out by x-ray. [7][22][36]  
Treat conservatively with ice for pain/swelling and an anti-inflammatory. [36]  
Start Motrin (ibuprofen) 800 mg three times daily with food. [36]  
If not improving over the next week or so, patient will contact the clinic for re-evaluation. [36]  
Orders for medications/procedures were placed as discussed. [38]
