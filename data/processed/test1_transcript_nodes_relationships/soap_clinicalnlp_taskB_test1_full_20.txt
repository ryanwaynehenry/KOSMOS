## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Gregory presents for evaluation of a nonhealing right foot ulcer present for ~2 months, recently becoming red with drainage. [0][1]  
He thinks it may have started after walking barefoot on the beach (Dewey Beach). [3][13]  
He reports intermittent throbbing but no specific pain, and his main concern is the new redness and drainage. [15][17]  
He saw his PCP previously and was prescribed an antibiotic, but he has not picked it up yet and does not recall the name. [4][5][7]  
He has diabetes, takes his medication, but does not check blood sugars regularly and is not sure how to do so. [19][21]  
He reports his last A1c was around 10. [23]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Vital signs were normal and he was afebrile. [24]  
Right foot exam: ~1 x 2 inch circular wound on the dorsal-lateral right foot just proximal to the 5th MTP with surrounding erythema, edema, and drainage/fluid. [24]  
No necrosis, no odor, and no bony exposure were appreciated, and clinician suspected infection. [24]  
Patient reported pressure with palpation but no distinct pain. [25]  

## RESULTS
Right foot X-ray showed no evidence of osteomyelitis (no bone infection). [26]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Diabetic foot ulcer of the right foot with clinical signs of local skin/soft tissue infection; no radiographic osteomyelitis. [24][26][26]  
Order ABI (ankle-brachial index) to assess blood supply and healing potential. [26]  
Perform debridement in clinic to remove dead tissue. [26]  
Start clindamycin 400 mg PO twice daily for 7 days for infection with redness/drainage. [26]  
Provide a surgical/offloading shoe to reduce pressure from regular shoes and have patient wear it for the next month. [28]  
Follow up in 2 weeks for reassessment and possible repeat debridement. [28]  
Advised patient to call the office if issues arise before follow-up, and to go to urgent care/ER for emergency symptoms such as fever/feeling unwell or if the foot becomes black. [32]  
Discussed that diabetes control is likely contributing to poor healing and recommended follow-up with PCP for a diabetes treatment plan given A1c ~10. [18][23][24]
