## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Worsening chronic headaches with tinnitus, intermittent peripheral blurred vision, and papilledema—presentation concerning for idiopathic intracranial hypertension (IIH). [4][6][8][9][10][25][28]  
Discussed that IIH involves increased intracranial pressure without a known cause and that ongoing elevated pressure can lead to permanent vision changes. [28]  
Plan to obtain a brain MRI to evaluate for other conditions contributing to symptoms, with insurance preapproval and scheduling to be arranged by the office team. [28][32]  
After MRI results, plan for lumbar puncture (spinal tap) to measure intracranial pressure and potentially provide symptomatic relief by removing CSF. [28]  
Referral to neuro-ophthalmology for a complete eye exam. [28]  
Discussed that surgery is not necessarily required, as medications may be tried to decrease intracranial pressure, and exercise/weight loss are important parts of treatment. [30]
