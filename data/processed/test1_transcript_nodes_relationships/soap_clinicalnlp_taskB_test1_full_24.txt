## RESULTS
Lumbar spine MRI showed an L4–L5 disc herniation with associated nerve root impingement/compression. [30]

[30] Given persistent symptoms despite prior NSAIDs and physical therapy, the clinician recommended an epidural steroid injection and planned to perform it today after discussing that it places anti-inflammatory medication at the site of inflammation and may take 2–3 weeks for full effect. [30] The patient agreed to proceed with the injection today. [31][30] After the injection, the plan is to refer the patient back to physical therapy to strengthen the area and to follow up with the clinician in about one month.
