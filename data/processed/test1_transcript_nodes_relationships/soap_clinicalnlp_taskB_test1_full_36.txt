## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Jerry is a 45-year-old male who presents with an ankle injury after landing awkwardly and rolling/twisting his ankle while playing basketball with his son. [0][1]  
He reports pain on the outside of the ankle with visible bruising and swelling. [1]  
He is having trouble walking; he can stand but cannot walk on it due to pain and a sense that it is not solid/stable. [3][13][14][15]  
He recently got back into working out and is concerned about disrupting his gym routine, and he also volunteered to coach starting in April. [3][5][17][19]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Right ankle with swelling laterally and exquisite lateral tenderness to palpation, with some tenderness over the medial deltoid region. [8][10][12]  
Skin intact without epidermolysis, brisk capillary refill, and no obvious malalignment. [12]  

## RESULTS
Right ankle x-ray (AP/lateral/oblique) shows a displaced lateral malleolus fracture at the Weber C level. [16]  
No evidence of medial or posterior malleolar fractures on x-ray. [16]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
**Displaced lateral malleolar fracture (Weber C), right ankle, with clinical instability and difficulty ambulating.** [13][16]  
Recommend operative management given fracture location/instability (likely plate and screws), and provide crutches. [16][22]  
Estimated time out from full activity is about 3 months; patient may continue coaching during recovery as tolerated. [17][22]  
Start meloxicam for pain/swelling and advise icing and elevation. [24]  
Patient works from home and can elevate the ankle regularly. [24][25]
