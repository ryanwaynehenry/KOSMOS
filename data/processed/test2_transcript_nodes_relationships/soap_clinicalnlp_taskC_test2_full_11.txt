Start spironolactone to help lower blood pressure in case of a hormonal driver (e.g., hyperaldosteronism) contributing to resistant hypertension. [45][47][48]  
Counseled on medication risks including electrolyte imbalance and dehydration; advised to maintain hydration and monitor for dizziness when standing. [49]  
Counseled that spironolactone can rarely raise potassium and can also contribute to low sodium when combined with hydrochlorothiazide, so labs are required. [49][51]

Follow up in 2 weeks for blood pressure check and metabolic panel/electrolytes (including sodium and potassium). [51][63][69][75]  
If not controlled on spironolactone, next steps include consideration of carvedilol (discussed with concern for erectile dysfunction) and/or referral to a resistant hypertension clinic for evaluation of secondary causes and possible procedural options (e.g., renal denervation). [63][65][67][75]
