## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Gregory presents with right ankle pain and swelling after slipping and falling on ice yesterday while taking out the trash. [3][4][5]  
Immediately after the fall he was unable to walk and needed a friend’s help to get up, but after resting, elevating, and icing overnight he can bear a bit more weight today though he is still limping. [6]  
He has been icing the ankle and taking ibuprofen, which he feels is helping. [7][8]  
He denies numbness in the foot. [25][26]  
He reports he plays a lot of sports (soccer) and does not think he has previously injured this ankle. [9][10][11][12]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Vital signs were reviewed and were normal. [27]  
Right ankle exam showed ecchymosis/bruising over the lateral malleolus with associated swelling and tenderness to palpation anterolaterally in the soft tissue. [27][28][29]  
There was no laxity on anterior drawer or inversion stress testing. [29]  
There was no bony tenderness on palpation of the foot. [29]  
Neurovascular exam of the right foot was intact with capillary refill <3 seconds, strong dorsalis pedis pulse, and sensation intact to light touch. [29]  

## RESULTS
Right ankle x-ray showed no fracture. [29]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Right ankle sprain after fall on ice, with bruising and swelling and negative x-ray for fracture. [3][4][27][29]  
Recommend continued elevation (especially when seated) and icing. [6][29]  
Provide an aircast for stabilization and crutches for the next 1–2 days, then begin walking/weight-bearing as tolerated. [31]  
Continue NSAIDs as needed for pain. [37]  
Expected significant improvement over the next ~2 weeks; plan follow-up in a few weeks to reassess and determine readiness to return to soccer. [37][39]  
Return precautions: call the clinic if any new or worsening symptoms occur before the next visit. [43]
