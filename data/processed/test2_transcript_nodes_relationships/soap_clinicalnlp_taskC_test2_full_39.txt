## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Angela presents with left knee pain after an injury about 1 week ago while running around with her 5-year-old niece who is learning to ride a bike without training wheels. [0][1][5][9]  
She reports she fell and twisted the knee and heard a “pop” at the time of injury. [3][13]  
Since then, she has been limping and feels the pain has not improved and is getting worse, described as throbbing. [9][15]  
Pain is on both sides of the knee but worse on the medial (inside) aspect. [11][12]  
Pain started around 4/10 and has worsened. [15]  
She has a history of a recent ulcer and was told not to take NSAIDs (Advil/ibuprofen). [17][23]  
She has been using Tylenol and kinesiology tape; Tylenol provides only brief partial relief with pain returning within 1–2 hours. [19][25]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Left knee exam notable for medial joint line tenderness to palpation. [28][29]  
There is edema and effusion of the left knee with moderate range of motion and pain with flexion and extension. [30]  
Lachman test is negative, and valgus/varus stress tests are negative. [30]  
Based on the exam, clinician felt there was no evidence of a tear. [30]  

## RESULTS
Left knee x-ray was normal with no fracture or bony abnormalities. [30]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Left knee pain felt most consistent with an MCL (medial collateral ligament) strain. [32]  
Start tramadol 50 mg twice daily (non-NSAID option) and continue Tylenol as needed for breakthrough pain. [32]  
Provide a knee immobilizer to wear for the next week to limit motion and help healing/reduce inflammation. [32]  
Ice the knee 3–4 times per day for 20–30 minutes to help swelling. [32]  
Activity modification: avoid running for the next 3 weeks; expected return to normal in about 1 month. [32][34]  
If symptoms worsen, patient should call the office for reassessment and possible further imaging (e.g., MRI). [34]
