## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Philip presents with right elbow pain after injuring it about 1 week ago while playing lacrosse with his kids, with onset while throwing the ball. [0][1] The pain is located at the tip/posterior aspect of the elbow and is sharp, worse with any movement, and radiates down the forearm. [5][7][13][15][17] He does not recall a fall but reports his child was checking him with a lacrosse stick and he may have been hit. [8][9][11] He tried Advil for a couple of days and thinks it helped somewhat, though he often forgets to take it. [19][21] He has a history of hypertension and diabetes and is taking lisinopril 20 mg daily and metformin 500 mg daily. [0][22][23][28][29] Home glucose checks (not daily) have been about 80–100. [31] He denies chest pain and abdominal pain. [32][33][34][35]

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Blood pressure in clinic was approximately 150/90. [22] Lungs were clear bilaterally. [36] Cardiac exam notable for known grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur. [36] Right elbow exam showed tenderness to palpation over the olecranon/posterior elbow with pain on flexion/extension but normal range of motion. [36][38][40]

## RESULTS
Right elbow x-ray (done prior to the visit) was normal with no fracture or bony abnormality. [40]

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
**1) Right elbow contusion (post-traumatic), with swelling/inflammation/bruising; x-ray negative.** [40] Start ibuprofen 800 mg twice daily for pain and swelling. [40] Ice the elbow 3 times daily for 20 minutes each session. [40] Avoid playing lacrosse for the next couple of weeks to allow healing, then may return to activity. [40]

**2) Hypertension, elevated in clinic (150/90) on lisinopril 20 mg daily; possible white coat effect; diet counseling.** [22][24][25] Continue lisinopril 20 mg daily and continue low-salt/Mediterranean-style diet. [22][25][40]

**3) Type 2 diabetes mellitus on metformin 500 mg daily with reported glucose 80–100 when checked.** [28][29][31] Order hemoglobin A1c to assess control and consider future metformin dose adjustment depending on results. [32][40]

Patient had no questions and agreed with the plan. [41]
