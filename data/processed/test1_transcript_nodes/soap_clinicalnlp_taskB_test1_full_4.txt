HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS  
Mrs. Martinez is a 43-year-old female presenting with pain in the distal radius area toward the wrist. [2][3][4]  
She reports the injury occurred while playing tennis when she attempted a backhand, missed the ball, and struck the top of the net/pole, which jarred her arm. [7]  
She noticed a small amount of swelling afterward. [8][9]  
She has a little pain with wrist movement and denies any numbness in the hand. [10][11]  
Past history is notable for allergies treated with Flonase. [12][13]  
She reports a prior traumatic injury when she fell during lawn work onto a rake. [14][15][17]  

PHYSICAL EXAM  
There is tenderness to palpation over the distal radius. [24][25][36]  
Pain is elicited with bending the arm. [26][27]  
Pain is less with moving the wrist “backwards” and is present a little with flexion, with the most pain during the provoking motion demonstrated on exam. [30][31][32][33][34][35]  
Pain is also provoked when stressing and flexing the thumb. [36]  

RESULTS  
Arm x-ray showed no fracture or other abnormality and was read as essentially normal. [22]  

ASSESSMENT AND PLAN  
Distal radius/forearm pain after tennis injury, assessed as muscle strain with possible contusion, with fracture ruled out by x-ray. [7][22][36]  
Treat conservatively with ice for pain/swelling and an anti-inflammatory. [36]  
Start Motrin (ibuprofen) 800 mg three times daily with food. [36]  
If not improving over the next week or so, patient should notify the clinician for re-evaluation. [36]
