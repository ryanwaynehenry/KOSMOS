## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Diabetic foot ulcer of the right foot with clinical signs of local infection (erythema, drainage, edema) and no radiographic osteomyelitis. [24][26]  
Plan: order ABI (ankle-brachial index) to assess blood supply and healing potential. [26]  
Plan: perform debridement today to remove dead tissue. [26]  
Plan: start clindamycin 400 mg PO twice daily for 7 days for infection. [26]  
Plan: provide a surgical/offloading shoe to reduce pressure from regular footwear and have him wear it for the next month. [26][28]  
Follow up in 2 weeks for reassessment and possible repeat debridement. [28]  
Diabetes mellitus, poorly controlled by history (A1c ~10) with limited home glucose monitoring; advised to follow up with PCP for a diabetes treatment plan and improved control to aid wound healing. [18][19][23][24]  
Return precautions: call the office if issues arise; go to urgent care/ER for emergency symptoms such as fever/feeling unwell or if the foot turns black. [32]
