Bobby presents with right shoulder pain for the past few weeks that began after doing multiple backyard/yard projects, with persistent soreness that has not resolved. [2][3][21]  
He reports a prior right shoulder injury from playing baseball when he was younger and has had some baseline issues since then. [5]  
Current pain is located more on the outside of the shoulder and is tender/worse when raising the arm. [7]  
He reports no restriction in shoulder movement but has pain with movement and minimal/no pain at rest. [9][11]  
Symptoms bother him mildly at night because he sleeps on that side. [13]  
He has been using Advil and icing, which help a little but the shoulder remains sore to touch. [17][19]  
He denies numbness in the arm or hands. [24][25]  
He also reports bilateral foot soreness and stiffness (including ankle joints) after extensive yard work, with some improvement using elevation and ice. [33]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Vital signs were normal, including T 97.8, HR 62, RR 16, BP 116/84, and SpO2 99%, and he was afebrile. [26]  
Right shoulder exam showed limited active and passive range of motion with tenderness at the greater tuberosity of the humerus and no tenderness at the sternoclavicular or acromioclavicular joints. [26]  
Grip strength was good. [26]

## RESULTS
Shoulder x-ray showed no fracture. [26]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Right shoulder pain, assessed as most consistent with rotator cuff tendinopathy. [26]  
Order MRI of the shoulder given prior shoulder problems and to evaluate for other pathology. [26]  
Refer to physical therapy for approximately 6–8 weeks. [26][28]  
Continue Tylenol for symptom control during the PT course. [26]  
If symptoms do not improve, consider a steroid injection for pain relief. [26]  
Bilateral foot/ankle soreness after yard work without clear injury: continue supportive care and try soaking feet in Epsom salts to help relax muscles and reduce soreness. [33][34]
