## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Jerry is a 45-year-old male who presents today for evaluation of an ankle injury sustained while playing basketball with his son. [0][1]  
He reports landing awkwardly after a shot and rolling/twisting his ankle, followed by pain on the outside of the ankle with visible bruising and swelling. [1]  
He reports trouble walking due to pain and a sense that the ankle does not feel solid/stable. [3][13][15]  
He is concerned about disruption to his recently resumed gym/workout routine and notes he also volunteered to coach starting in April. [3][5][17][19]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Right ankle exam showed swelling on the lateral side with exquisite lateral tenderness and some tenderness over the medial deltoid region. [8][10][12]  
Skin was intact with no epidermolysis, capillary refill was brisk, and there was no obvious malalignment. [12]  
Patient was able to stand but reported he could not walk on it due to pain and instability. [13][14]  

## RESULTS
Right ankle x-ray (AP/lateral/oblique) demonstrated a displaced lateral malleolus fracture at the Weber C level. [16]  
There was no evidence of medial or posterior malleolar fracture on x-ray. [16]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
**1) Displaced right lateral malleolus fracture (Weber C), unstable** — Diagnosed based on exam and x-ray findings, with instability symptoms when walking. [12][13][16]  
Plan: Recommend operative management (likely ORIF with plate and screws) given fracture location/instability. [16]  
Plan: Provide crutches and advise activity restriction; discussed expected time out of activity of about 3 months while healing, with goal to return to exercise thereafter. [16][22]  

**2) Pain/swelling management** — Start meloxicam, and advise icing and elevation to reduce swelling. [24]  
Patient reports he works from home and should be able to elevate the ankle regularly. [24][25]
