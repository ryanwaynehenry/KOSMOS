## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Jack presents for evaluation of neck pain after a motor vehicle accident in which he was hit from behind and his neck/head moved forward quickly. [0][1][3]  
The accident occurred about 1 week ago. [4][5]  
He reports posterior neck pain rated 7/10 that is not constant but is triggered by moving his head/neck. [6][7][9][11]  
He tried ibuprofen with partial relief (down to about 5/10). [12][13][15]  
Symptoms have been staying about the same overall and worsen with neck movement. [16][17]  
He can mostly turn his neck side to side but it is painful. [18][19]  
He had a couple headaches early on after the accident but has not had headaches lately. [20][21][23]  
He denies radiation of pain to the upper back or shoulders. [24][25]  
He denies hearing problems or visual disturbances. [26][27]  
He played football years ago but did not have prior neck issues. [28][29][31]  
He did not go to the emergency room after the accident due to cost concerns. [32][33]  
He is not aware of any neck swelling or bruising. [34][35]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Neck: mild tenderness/pain to palpation of the soft tissues on the side of the neck. [36][37][38]  
Back/shoulders: no pain with palpation. [38][39]  
Neck range of motion: pain with flexion (chin to chest), worse pain with extension, and pain with rotation. [40][41][42][43][44][45]  
Neck lateral bending is painful, and he is unable to touch his ear to his shoulder. [46][47][48]  

## RESULTS
X-ray was reviewed and showed no signs of fracture or bony abnormalities. [48]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Neck pain after rear-impact motor vehicle accident, assessed as neck strain/whiplash. [1][3][48]  
Treat conservatively with anti-inflammatories: Motrin (ibuprofen) 600 mg every 6–8 hours. [48]  
Start muscle relaxant: Flexeril (cyclobenzaprine) 10 mg every 12 hours as needed. [48]  
Activity advice: avoid further neck strain and be conservative with neck movements. [48]  
Order MRI given he did not go to the hospital after the accident, to further evaluate and to help guide next steps. [48]  
Plan referral to physical rehabilitation/physical therapy or chiropractor after MRI results, with physical therapy as an alternative if he prefers not to see a chiropractor. [48][49][50]
