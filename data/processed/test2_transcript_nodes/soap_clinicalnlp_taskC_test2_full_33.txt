## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Kevin is an 82-year-old male with a history of hypertension and stage 3 chronic kidney disease presenting for hospital follow-up after an acute on chronic CHF exacerbation. [0]  
He reports that despite trying to watch his diet, he developed ankle swelling and then became very short of breath, prompting a 911 call; paramedics placed a breathing mask, and in the ER he was told his blood pressure was very high. [1]  
The clinician reviewed that his blood pressure in the hospital/ER was 200/90 and the patient reports imperfect adherence to his medications, sometimes missing 1–2 doses. [2][3]  
He reports dietary “slips,” including eating pizza and chicken wings at weekend parties with friends. [5][6]  
He was hospitalized for a couple of days and received IV diuretics (“water pill”) with significant diuresis and reports he feels much better now and is on different blood pressure medications. [9]  
Since discharge, he reports taking medications and following instructions because he wants to avoid rehospitalization. [11]  
He obtained a home blood pressure cuff and has been checking BP about every other day, reporting readings have been “really good.” [13]  
Since being home, he denies shortness of breath, sleeping problems (aside from taking afternoon naps), and chest pain. [15][17]

Daily weights and call if weight increases by ~3 lb in 2 days. [22]
