## RESULTS
Lumbar spine MRI (obtained prior to the visit) shows disc desiccation, disc bulge with paracentral disc herniation, and moderate right neural foraminal stenosis consistent with nerve impingement. [62]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Low back pain at L4–L5 due to herniated disc with radiculopathy (radiating leg pain) based on symptoms, exam, and MRI findings. [13][58][62]  
Discussed that the disc bulge is pinching nerves, explaining the radiating leg pain. [62]  
Plan: proceed with a lumbar spinal injection using a strong NSAID and a corticosteroid under fluoroscopic guidance to reduce inflammation and pain. [62]  
Patient requested to have the injection done today, and clinician stated it can be performed today. [63][64]  
Reviewed that there are risks/complications with the injection as with any procedure, and the team will provide paperwork with explanations and answer additional questions. [67][68]
