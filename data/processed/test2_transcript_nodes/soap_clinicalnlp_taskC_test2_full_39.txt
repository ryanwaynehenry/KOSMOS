## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Angela presents with left knee pain after falling and twisting her knee while running around with her niece (who is learning to ride a bike without training wheels) about 1 week ago. [0][1][3][9]  
Since the injury, she has been limping and feels the pain has not improved and is getting worse. [9][15]  
Pain is on both sides of the knee but worse on the medial (inside) aspect, and she reports hearing a “pop” at the time of injury. [11][13]  
Pain started around 4/10 and has become more throbbing over time. [15]  
She cannot take NSAIDs (e.g., Advil/ibuprofen) due to a recent ulcer and has been using Tylenol and kinesiology tape, with only mild/temporary relief (pain returns within 1–2 hours). [17][19][25]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Left knee: tenderness on the medial aspect to palpation. [28][29]  
Left knee: edema and effusion present with moderate range of motion and pain with flexion/extension. [30]  
Stability testing: negative Lachman, and negative valgus/varus stress tests. [30]  

## RESULTS
Left knee x-ray was normal with no fracture or bony abnormality. [30]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Left knee pain—suspected MCL (medial collateral ligament) strain based on mechanism, medial pain, and reassuring stability tests with normal x-ray. [3][11][30][32]  
Start tramadol 50 mg twice daily (non-NSAID option) and continue Tylenol as needed for breakthrough pain, with counseling to avoid excessive Tylenol due to liver risk. [32][26]  
Provide a knee immobilizer to wear for the next week to limit motion and help healing/reduce inflammation. [32]  
Ice the knee 3–4 times per day for 20–30 minutes to help swelling. [32]  
Activity modification: avoid running for the next ~3 weeks; expected return to normal in about 1 month if improving. [32][34]  
Return precautions: if symptoms worsen, call the office for reassessment and possible further imaging (MRI). [34]
