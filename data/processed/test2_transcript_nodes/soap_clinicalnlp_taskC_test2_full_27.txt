She previously took antibiotics (Keflex) with some improvement but the wound has not resolved; last antibiotic course was about 5 months ago. [7][8][9][11]  
She reports no pain but does have decreased sensation in the foot, consistent with diabetic neuropathy. [12][13][14]  
Recently she has noticed increased drainage from the wound and has felt more tired. [14][15]  
Her blood sugars have improved but are still in the 200s, and her last A1c was about 10; she was advised to follow up with her PCP for diabetes control. [19][20]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Vitals were reassuring and she was afebrile. [22]  
Right foot: ~1 x 2 cm circular wound on the dorsal lateral right foot just proximal to the 5th MTP joint with yellow slough and minimal granulation tissue. [22]  
No surrounding erythema/cellulitis, no fluid collection, no necrosis, no odor, and no bony exposure were noted. [22]  
The wound was not painful to palpation. [22][23]  

## RESULTS
Right foot x-ray showed no evidence of osteomyelitis (no bone infection). [24]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
Nonhealing diabetic foot ulcer of the right dorsal lateral foot: order ankle-brachial index (ABI) to assess blood supply and healing potential. [24]  
Perform in-office debridement to remove nonviable tissue. [24][26]  
Start collagenase ointment applied once daily to the wound and cover with a sterile dressing, continuing until the wound begins to shrink. [26]  
Provide a surgical shoe for offloading and advise minimizing pressure on the area, especially with anticipated walking this weekend. [26][28]  
Follow up in 2 weeks for reassessment and possible repeat debridement. [28][30]  
Type 2 diabetes mellitus with hyperglycemia (A1c ~10): patient advised to reconnect with PCP to improve glycemic control to support wound healing. [19][20]
