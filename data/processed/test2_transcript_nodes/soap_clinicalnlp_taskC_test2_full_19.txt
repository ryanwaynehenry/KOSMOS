## HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Matthew is a 67-year-old right-handed man who presents with right shoulder pain after an injury while playing tackle football. [0][8][9][11]  
About 4 days ago (last weekend), he was tackled and landed on his right shoulder “weird,” and since then his shoulder has been bothering him without improvement. [4][5][7][24][25]  
Pain is located on the posterior aspect of the right shoulder. [16][17]  
He reports pain with lifting the shoulder up toward the ear and is avoiding heavy lifting; reaching down to pick up a box was difficult, though he can still grip and hold a cup of coffee. [14][15][18][19]  
He denies numbness/tingling in the fingers and denies weakness affecting hand function. [12][13][14][15]  
He has tried Tylenol with minimal benefit (kept pain from worsening but did not reduce it). [20][21][22][23]  
He denies neck pain/injury and denies other joint pain in the elbow or wrist on that side. [26][27][28][29]  
He denies prior injury to that shoulder. [30][31]  
Past surgical history includes appendectomy for appendicitis about 3–4 years ago. [32][33][35]  
Since the injury, he reports a slight headache, notes he hit his head a little, and denies loss of consciousness; he denies fever. [37][39][41][42]  

## PHYSICAL EXAM
Vital signs: afebrile, BP 122/76, HR 82, SpO₂ 95% on room air. [42]  
Cervical spine: no tenderness to palpation and full range of motion. [42]  
Head: no apparent trauma on exam. [42]  
Right shoulder: no ecchymosis/bruising; tenderness to palpation at the posterior shoulder/AC joint with decreased abduction/adduction to ~90 degrees and pain with supination/pronation. [42]  
Neurovascular: palpable radial pulse. [42]  

## RESULTS
Right shoulder X-ray (performed prior to clinician evaluation) showed an acute acromioclavicular (AC) joint fracture (small fracture of the acromion). [42]  

## ASSESSMENT AND PLAN
1) **Acute right acromioclavicular joint fracture (small acromion fracture) with persistent pain and limited ROM after tackle football injury**. [4][5][7][42]  
- Immobilize with a sling for now. [42]  
- Start meloxicam 15 mg once daily for pain/inflammation; patient does not feel he needs anything stronger at this time. [42][44][45]  
- Order CT scan of the right shoulder to better evaluate the joint. [42]  
- Refer to physical therapy to strengthen the shoulder with the goal of avoiding surgery; clinician does not think surgery will be needed currently. [42]  

2) **Return-to-play counseling**. [49][50][52]  
- No tackle football for a while; must allow fracture to heal, and clinician advised avoiding tackle football in the future (consider flag football instead). [50][52][53]
